## Key Agencies/Sources to Cite or Approach for Official Data

- **Lagos State Ministry of Housing** — housing.lagosstate.gov.ng
- **Lagos State Development and Property Corporation (LSDPC)** — lsdpc.gov.ng (builds/manages many estates above)
- **Lagos State Mortgage Board (Lagos HOMS)** — lagoshoms.lagosstate.gov.ng / lagoshoms.gov.ng
- **Federal Ministry of Housing & Urban Development** — fmhud.gov.ng (for federal-state joint projects like Renewed Hope)

## Important Caveats for the Pitch

1. **This list is incomplete and secondhand** — compiled from news articles, blogs, and aggregator sites (Ballers.ng, Propsult, Estate Intel), not primary government records. Present it as “here’s what’s publicly discoverable” — not as your proprietary dataset.
1. **No single source lists all estates** — this is exactly the fragmentation problem your pitch should highlight. You had to piece this together from 8+ different sites; a citizen or investor would have the same problem.
1. **Unit counts and status are inconsistent** — some sources report unit numbers, most don’t; “completed” status is self-reported by news coverage, not verified.
1. **Illegal/unapproved estates are a separate, larger dataset** — Lagos State listed 176 unapproved private estates in 2025 enforcement action. This is a different (private-sector) issue but shows the Ministry already tracks approval status somewhere — a hook for “let’s make this queryable.”
